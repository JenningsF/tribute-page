Tom Hanks

America's Dad

Timeline of Tom Hank's life and career

  • 1956 — Born in Concord, California.
  • 1974 — Graduated Skyline HS in Oakland, California before attending Chabot College in Hawyard, California.
  • 1976 — Transferred to California State University, Sacremento to study theater.
  • 1978 — Dropped out of college to pursue theater and interned at the Great Lakes Theater Festival in Cleveland, Ohio.
  • 1978 — Married actress Samantha Lewes and had two children, Colin Hanks (born 1977) and Elizabeth Hanks (born 1982).
  • 1979 — Moved to New York City to continue theater and branch into film.
  • 1980 — First movie credit playing Elliot in He Knows You're Alone
  • 1980 — First lead television role playing Kip Wilson on Bosom Buddies
  • 1987 — Divorced Samantha Lewes.
  • 1988 — Stared as lead character, Josh, in Big which earned his first Academy Award nomination.
  • 1988 — Married actress, singer, songwriter, and producer, Rita Wilson, which they have two children, Chester "Chet" Hanks (born 1990) and Truman Hanks (born 1995).
  • 1989 — Only financially successful movie of the '80s, Turner & Hooch is released.
  • 1992 — A League of Their Own is released, where Hanks portrays Jimmy Dugan, a washed-up baseball legend turned manager.
  • 1993 — Stars in Sleepless in Seattle and Philadelphia, winning the 1993 Academy Award for Best Actor for his role in Philadelphia.
  • 1994 — Wins second Best Actor Academy Award for his role in Forrest Gump becoming only the second actor to have accomplished the feat of winning consecutive Best Actor Oscars.
  • 1995 — Depicts astronaut and commander Jim Lovell in Apollo 13 and voices toy cowboy Woody in Toy Story, which earned a combined 13 Academy Award Nominations.
  • 1996 — Directoral debut with That Thing You Do!
  • 1998 — Nominated for first two Emmy Awards and won Outstanding Limited Series for From the Earth to the Moon.
  • 1998 — Received another Best Actor Academy Award nomination for his role in Saving Private Ryan and also had cement prints placed in front of the Grauman's Chinese Theatre in Hollywood.
  • 1999 — Reprised voice of Woody in Toy Story 2 and starred in an adaptation of the Stephen King novel The Green Mile.
  • 2000 — Earned fourth Golden Globe win for Best Actor and fifth Academy Award nomination for Best Actor as Chuck Noland in Cast Away.
  • 2001 — Hanks helped direct and produce the Emmy Award-winning HBO miniseries Band of Brothers.
  • 2002 — Starred opposite Leonardo DiCaprio in the hit biographical crime drama Me If You Can.
  • 2002 — Became the youngest-ever recipient of the American Film Institute's Life Achievement Award at the age of 45.
  • 2006 — Starred as Robert Langdon in the film adaptation to Dan Brown's novel, The Da Vinci Code.
  • 2006 — Received the Douglas S. Morrow Public Outreach Award.
  • 2009 — Reprised role as Robert Langdon Angels & Demons, also based on the novel of the same name by Dan Brown.
  • 2010 — Played the voice of Woody again for Toy Story 3, which went on to become the first animated film to gross a worldwide total of over $1 billion as well as the highest-grossing animated film at the time.
  • 2013 — Starred in two critically acclaimed films Captain Phillips and Saving Mr. Banks.
  • 2013 — Made Broadway debut starring in Lucky Guy, which earned a Tony Award nomination for Best Actor in a Play.
  • 2014 — Received Kennedy Center Honors Medallion.
  • 2015 — Portrayed lawyer James B. Donovan in Bridge of Spies.
  • 2016 — Received the Presidential Medal of Freedom, which is the highest civilian award given by the President of the United States.
  • 2016 — Received the French Legion of Honor, which is the highest French order of merit, for his presentation of World War II and support of World War II veterans.
  • 2016 — Starred in Clint Eastwood's Sully, playing airline captain Chelsey Sullenberger landing a flight in the Hudson River and reprised his role as Robert Langdon in the third and final movie, Inferno.
  • 2019 — Became an honorary citizen of Greece.
  • 2019 — Reprised role as the voice of Woody in Toy Story 4 and earned his tenth Golden Globe and sixth Academy Award nomination for his role as Fred Rogers in A Beautiful Day in the Neighborhood.
  • 2020 — Received the Golden Glober Cecil B. Demille Award.
  • 2020 — Starred in Greyhound and his first Western film, News of the World.
